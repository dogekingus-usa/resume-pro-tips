# How to Write a Federal Resume (USAJobs Guide)

May 2026 · 10 min read · ' Resume Tips

## Federal Resumes: A Different Breed
Federal resumes submitted through USAJobs.gov follow different rules than corporate resumes. They're typically longer (5+ pages), more detailed, and must follow specific formatting guidelines. The federal hiring process uses a different evaluation system that prioritizes comprehensive documentation over brevity.


## Key Differences from Corporate Resumes
**Length:** While corporate resumes should be 1-2 pages, federal resumes routinely run 5-7 pages. Federal HR specialists expect detailed descriptions of every position you've held, including duties, accomplishments, and specific examples of your work.


**Format:** Use the USAJobs Resume Builder to ensure compatibility with the federal application system. Pay attention to formatting requirements: specific fonts, margins, and section headers may be required.


**Keywords:** Federal job announcements include specific keywords and phrases that your resume must mirror. These often relate to specific regulations, policies, or competencies required for the role.


## The KSAs: Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities
Many federal positions require you to demonstrate specific KSAs. Address each KSA directly with concrete examples from your experience. Use the same language and terminology found in the job announcement. Each KSA response should be a complete paragraph with specific examples.


## Tips for Federal Resume Success
- Use the exact job title and grade level from the announcement- Include your supervisor's contact information for each role- List your salary range for each position- Provide hours worked per week for each job- Include relevant training, certifications, and awards
