# How to Write a Functional Resume for Career Changers

May 2026 · 7 min read · ?? Resume Tips

## Why Career Changers Need a Different Approach
When you are changing careers, your chronological resume tells the wrong story. A functional resume flips this narrative by putting transferable skills front and center. This format emphasizes what you can do, not where you did it.


## Structure of a Career Changer Resume
Professional Summary: Frame your career change as an asset. Relevant Skills Section: List 3-5 skill categories with specific examples. Brief Work History: Include condensed version with focus on transferable achievements. Education and Certifications: Highlight any training relevant to your new field.


## Making the Case
Make explicit connections between past experience and future goals. Do not assume the reader will connect the dots. Managed 2M budget becomes relevant for financial analyst roles. Led cross-functional teams of 15 becomes relevant for project management.
