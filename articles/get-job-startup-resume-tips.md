## Startups Hire Differently
Startups evaluate candidates on different criteria than corporate employers. While large companies prioritize years of experience and specific credentials, startups value versatility, speed, ownership mentality, and cultural fit above all else. Your startup resume needs to emphasize these qualities.


## What Startup Founders Want to See
**Versatility:** Startups can't afford specialists who only do one thing. Highlight projects where you wore multiple hats, learned new skills quickly, and contributed outside your primary role. "At my last startup, I handled not just product management but also customer support, sales calls, and wrote technical documentation."


**Ownership Mentality:** Show that you take initiative and own outcomes rather than just completing assigned tasks. Use language like "I identified," "I built from scratch," "I took ownership of."


**Resourcefulness:** Demonstrate how you achieved results with limited resources. Startups value candidates who can do more with less and find creative solutions without waiting for permission or budget.


## Startup Resume Structure
Lead with a "Startup Experience" section or highlight early-stage company experience. Emphasize impact metrics even if they're smaller in scale than corporate metrics ' a 50% improvement at a startup is more impressive than a 10% improvement at a large company. Show that you thrive in ambiguity.
