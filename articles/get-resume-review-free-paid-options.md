# How to Get a Resume Review (Free and Paid Options)

May 2026 · 6 min read · ' Resume Tips

## Why a Resume Review Matters
A fresh pair of eyes can catch errors, inconsistencies, and missed opportunities in your resume. Professional resume reviewers understand what recruiters and ATS systems look for, and they can transform an average resume into one that consistently lands interviews. In 2026, both free and paid options are available depending on your budget and needs.


## Free Resume Review Options
**University Career Centers:** If you're a student or alumni, your university's career center offers free resume reviews with trained counselors. They understand what entry-level and mid-level recruiters expect.


**Online Communities:** Subreddits like r/resumes and r/jobs offer peer reviews. Post your resume with personal information redacted and receive feedback from experienced professionals.


**AI-Powered Tools:** Tools like Resume Worded, Jobscan, and TopResume's free review use AI to analyze your resume against job descriptions and provide actionable improvement suggestions.


## Paid Resume Review Services
**Professional Resume Writers:** Certified professional resume writers (CPRW, NCRW) offer in-depth reviews starting at $100-300. They provide line-by-line edits, formatting recommendations, and keyword optimization advice.


**Career Coaches:** Many career coaches include resume review as part of their services. One-hour sessions typically cost $150-400 and include verbal feedback plus written recommendations.


**Specialized Review Services:** Industry-specific services charge $50-150 for a targeted review. For example, a tech resume reviewer understands the specific keywords and formats that tech recruiters prefer.


## What to Look for in a Review
- ATS formatting compatibility- Keyword density and relevance- Quantified achievements and metrics- Consistency in verb tense and formatting- Overall narrative and career progression
