Networking Doesn't Have to Be Awkward

Many professionals avoid LinkedIn networking because it feels inauthentic or transactional. But effective networking is about building genuine professional relationships, not collecting contacts or sending generic messages. The key is to approach networking with a mindset of curiosity and mutual benefit rather than immediate self-interest.

The Right Way to Connect

When sending a connection request, always include a personalized note. Reference something specific: a shared connection, a post they wrote, an article they shared, or their professional background. "I really enjoyed your recent article on agile transformation. I'm a product manager transitioning from healthcare tech and would love to connect."

Engage Before You Ask

Build the relationship before making any requests. Like and comment on their posts, share their content with your network, and participate in discussions they're involved in. After several genuine interactions, your connection request will feel natural rather than transactional.

What to Offer

Networking is a two-way street. Consider what you can offer: industry insights, introductions to your network, feedback on their work, or simply thoughtful engagement with their content.
