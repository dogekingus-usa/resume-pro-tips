The Power of a Well-Written Summary

Your LinkedIn summary (the "About" section) is one of the few places on the platform where you can tell your professional story in your own words. Recruiters use this section to quickly assess your fit before sending an InMail. A compelling summary can double your inbound recruiter messages.

Summary Template 1: The Value Proposition

Formula: [Who you are] + [What you do] + [Who you help] + [Key results] + [Call to action]. Example: "Senior Product Manager helping B2B SaaS companies achieve product-market fit. Led 3 products from zero to $10M+ ARR. Ex-Microsoft, MBA. Open to CPG and health tech opportunities."

Summary Template 2: The Storyteller

Open with a compelling anecdote that illustrates your passion for your field. Follow with 2-3 key achievements that demonstrate your impact. Close with what you're looking for next. This format works well for creative roles, marketing, and leadership positions.

Summary Template 3: The Problem Solver

Start by naming a specific problem in your industry or field. Explain how you've helped solve it for previous employers or clients. List specific skills and approaches you bring to bear. End with an invitation to connect with professionals facing similar challenges.

What to Include in Every Summary

  • Your current role and expertise area
  • 2-3 quantifiable achievements
  • Industry-specific keywords
  • Your professional goals or what you're seeking
  • A call to action encouraging connection
