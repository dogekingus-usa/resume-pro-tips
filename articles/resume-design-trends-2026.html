Design Matters More Than Ever in 2026

With AI-powered ATS systems becoming more sophisticated, resume design plays a dual role: it must be machine-readable AND human-appealing. The best designs in 2026 strike a balance between visual appeal and technical compatibility. Here's what's working and what's not.

What's In for 2026

Clean Minimalism: Plenty of white space, clear section headers, and a logical visual hierarchy. Recruiters should be able to find key information within 3-5 seconds of scanning.

Subtle Color Accents: A single color used sparingly for headers, lines, or section dividers. Navy, forest green, and charcoal are popular choices. No more than one accent color total.

Data Visualization: Simple bar charts, progress bars, or skill meters when appropriate â€” but only if they add real value and don't confuse ATS systems.

Hybrid Layouts: Combining a traditional left-column structure with a sidebar for skills and contact information, similar to a well-designed dashboard.

What's Out for 2026

  • Two-column layouts that confuse ATS scanners
  • Infographics, icons, and complex graphics
  • Fancy fonts, cursive, or handwriting styles
  • Photo headshots on resumes (remains optional in most countries except where required)
  • Tables and text boxes that disrupt text extraction
