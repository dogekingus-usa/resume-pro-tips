## You've Made It to Round Two
Congratulations ' the first interview confirmed you're qualified. The second interview digs deeper to assess your fit, problem-solving abilities, and cultural alignment. This is where candidates who prepared for the first round but skimped on the second often stumble.


## What Changes in Round Two
**Deeper Technical Assessment:** Expect more challenging questions about your specific skills and experience. You may be asked to solve problems, analyze case studies, or present your approach to real challenges the team is facing.


**Multiple Interviewers:** Round two typically involves meeting 3-5 people: potential teammates, cross-functional partners, and sometimes a senior leader. Each interviewer evaluates you from a different angle.


**Culture Fit Assessment:** Interviewers will probe your working style, values, and how you handle conflict, feedback, and collaboration. Be prepared with specific examples that demonstrate cultural alignment.


## How to Prepare
**Research Your Interviewers:** Look up each person on LinkedIn. Understand their role, background, and what they care about. Prepare questions tailored to each person's perspective.


**Prepare Your Stories:** Review your STAR stories and map them to the deeper questions round two typically brings. Prepare for scenario-based questions specific to their industry or challenges.


**Come With Questions:** Ask about team dynamics, project priorities, success metrics, and growth opportunities. Your questions signal what you value and how you think.
