ATS-Friendly Resume Formatting: The Complete Guide for 2026

Published: May 16, 2026 | Reading time: 9 minutes

What Is an ATS and Why Should You Care?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software that companies use to manage the flood of resumes they receive for every job opening. When you submit your application, the ATS scans, parses, and scores your resume before a human recruiter ever lays eyes on it. In 2026, over 75% of large companies and 50% of mid-sized businesses use some form of ATS to filter candidates.

If your resume isn't formatted to be ATS-friendly, it may be discarded before a recruiter even sees it â€” regardless of how qualified you are. The good news? With the right formatting, you can ensure your resume sails through every automated gatekeeper.

How ATS Software Reads Your Resume

Before we dive into formatting rules, it helps to understand how ATS platforms work. Most systems use a parser that extracts text from your resume and drops it into a database. The parser looks for specific fields: name, contact info, work experience, education, skills, and certifications.

Problems arise when the parser can't interpret your layout. Columns, tables, text boxes, graphics, and unusual fonts can confuse the parser, causing it to jumble your information or miss it entirely. The simpler your layout, the better the parser handles it.

Top 10 ATS Formatting Rules

1. Use Standard Section Headings

Stick with conventional section labels that every ATS recognizes: Summary, Professional Experience, Work History, Education, Skills, Certifications. Avoid creative headings like "Where I've Been" or "What I Bring to the Table" â€” the ATS won't know what to do with them.

2. Choose a Simple, Single-Column Layout

Multi-column layouts may look sleek, but they wreak havoc on ATS parsers. The parser reads left-to-right, top-to-bottom, so columns can mix up your content. Stick to a single-column chronological or hybrid layout. This is the single most impactful change you can make for ATS compatibility.

3. Save as .docx or .pdf (But Test First)

Most modern ATS platforms handle .docx files perfectly. PDFs are increasingly well-supported, but some older systems still struggle. If the job posting doesn't specify a format, .docx is the safest bet. Always run your resume through an ATS simulator to verify the output.

4. Avoid Headers and Footers

Many ATS parsers fail to read text placed inside headers and footers of Word documents. Put your name, phone number, email, and LinkedIn URL in the main body of the page, not in the header or footer zone.

5. Use Standard Fonts at 10-12 Points

Stick with universally readable fonts: Arial, Calibri, Helvetica, Georgia, or Times New Roman. Font size should be 10-12 points for body text and 14-16 for your name. Unusual fonts may not render correctly and could produce garbled text.

6. Skip Graphics, Icons, and Images

ATS parsers cannot read text embedded in images. That beautiful infographic of your skills? Invisible to the ATS. Keep your resume text-based. If you want to include a headshot (not recommended in the US), place it in your LinkedIn profile, not your resume file.

7. Include a Skills Section with Keywords

The ATS scores your resume by matching keywords from the job description. Create a dedicated Skills section that lists both hard skills (software, tools, languages) and soft skills (leadership, communication, project management). Use the exact terminology from the job posting whenever possible.

8. Use Full Job Titles and Company Names

Don't abbreviate unless the abbreviation is the standard name (e.g., IBM, Google). If the job description says "Project Manager," use "Project Manager" â€” not "PM." The ATS looks for exact matches.

9. Avoid Tables and Text Boxes

Word-processor tables and text boxes confuse parsers. Use simple line breaks and spacing for layout instead. If your resume template uses a table to align dates on the right, replace it with tab stops or simple spacing.

10. Keep File Names Professional

Name your file something like "Jane-Doe-Resume-2026.docx" â€” not "Resume_FINAL_v3_copy(2).docx." Some ATS systems display the file name, and a clean file name creates a better impression before your content is even read.

ATS Resume Template: The Ideal Structure

  • Contact Information â€” Name, phone, email, LinkedIn URL, location (city, state)
  • Professional Summary â€” 2-3 sentences with your top qualifications and target role
  • Core Competencies / Skills â€” Bullet-point list of 10-15 relevant skills
  • Professional Experience â€” Reverse chronological order with company, title, dates, and 4-6 bullet points per role
  • Education â€” Degree, school, graduation year
  • Certifications (if applicable) â€” Name of certification, issuing body, date

Why ATS Formatting Matters More Than Ever in 2026

Artificial intelligence has made ATS systems smarter â€” but also stricter. Modern ATS platforms use machine learning to rank candidates, not just filter for keywords. If your resume is poorly formatted, the AI model may struggle to extract the data it needs for proper scoring.

Companies are also using ATS data to build talent pools for future openings. Even if you don't get this job, a properly parsed resume stays in the database and may surface for the next opportunity. Make sure your digital resume asset works for you long-term.
