How to Use Your Resume Bundle as a Recent Graduate

Step 1: Choose Your Base Template

Start with the "Entry-Level" or "Recent Graduate" template from the bundle. These are pre-optimized for candidates with less than 3 years of full-time experience. The formatting emphasizes your education, internships, projects, and relevant coursework.

Step 2: Identify Your Target Keywords

Review the job descriptions for roles you're targeting. Pull out 8-10 key skills, tools, and qualifications that appear repeatedly. Use the action verb database to integrate these naturally into your bullet points.

Step 3: Quantify Everything You Can

Even without professional experience, you can quantify your impact. Use numbers from internships, class projects, volunteer work, and student organizations:

  • "Led a team of 5 in a semester-long consulting project, delivering recommendations that reduced costs by 12%"
  • "Managed social media accounts reaching 3,000+ students, increasing event attendance by 40%"
  • "Analyzed 10,000+ data points using Python and Excel for a thesis on consumer behavior"
Pro Tip: Recent graduates who quantify their achievements on resumes get 40% more interview callbacks than those who list responsibilities only.

Step 4: Pair Your Resume with a Cover Letter

The bundle's cover letter templates are designed to match your resume's tone and structure. Use the cover letter to tell the story behind your resume — why you're passionate about the industry, what you learned in school, and how you'll add value from day one.
