ATS Resume Template: The 7 Non-Negotiable Sections That Get You an Interview in 2026

If you've been applying to jobs and hearing nothing back, the problem is almost certainly not your experienceit's your resume structure. In 2026, over 98% of Fortune 500 companies and the majority of mid-size employers use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to screen resumes before a human ever sees them. If your resume is missing even one of the sections an ATS expects, you're essentially invisible.

This guide breaks down the 7 non-negotiable resume sections that every ATS-optimized resume must include. Use this as your template, and you'll dramatically increase your chances of landing an interview.

Why These 7 Sections? The Science Behind ATS Parsing

Before we dive into the template, it's important to understand why these seven sections are non-negotiable. Applicant Tracking Systems parse resumes by scanning for standard section headers. When an ATS encounters a header like "Professional Experience," it knows exactly where to file that information. When it sees "My Work Journey" or "What I've Done," the parser gets confusedand your resume gets categorized as "unparseable," which often means automatic rejection.

According to a 2025 study by Jobscan, resumes using standard section headers have a 73% higher pass rate through ATS screening compared to resumes with creative or unconventional headers.

The seven sections below represent the universal standard recognized by every major ATS platform: Workday, Taleo, Lever, Greenhouse, BambooHR, and iCIMS.

Section 1: Contact Information & Header

What to include: Full name, phone number, professional email address, LinkedIn profile URL, and location (city, state).

Your header is the first thing both the ATS and recruiters see. Keep it clean and standard. Here are the rules:

  • Use your full legal name at the top in 16-18pt font. No nicknames unless you go by one professionally.
  • Email: Use a professional address. Firstname.lastname@gmail.com is fine. Avoid outdated emails like partyboy99@hotmail.com.
  • Phone: Include your number with area code. Make sure your voicemail greeting is professional.
  • LinkedIn: Always include a customized LinkedIn URL (linkedin.com/in/yourname). Recruiters check this.
  • Location: City and state is sufficient. No need for your full street addressprivacy risk and irrelevant to ATS.
ATS Tip: Do NOT put your contact information in the header/footer area of Word or Google Docs. Many ATS systems fail to read content placed in document headers and footers. Put it at the top of the body instead.

Section 2: Professional Summary

What to include: 3-4 sentences summarizing your experience, key skills, and what you bring to the role.

Your professional summary is your elevator pitch. It appears right below your contact header and serves as a quick snapshot for both the ATS and hiring managers. In 2026, this section is more important than ever because many ATS platforms now use AI-based candidate matching that analyzes your summary against the job description.

How to write it:

  • Lead with your title: "Results-driven Marketing Manager with 8+ years of experience in B2B SaaS."
  • Include 2-3 core skills: Mention your strongest, most relevant abilities using keywords from the job description.
  • Add a quantifiable achievement: "Increased organic traffic by 150% year-over-year across three product lines."
  • State your goal: "Seeking to leverage expertise in demand generation to drive growth at a Series B tech company."
ATS Tip: Include the exact job title you're applying for in your summary when it matches your background. ATS algorithms weigh title alignment heavily in ranking.

?? Professional Summary Template

[Job Title] with [X+] years of experience in [industry/field]. Proven track record of [key achievement 1] and [key achievement 2]. Skilled in [skill 1], [skill 2], and [skill 3]. Looking to bring [core strength] to a [type of company] role.

Section 3: Core Competencies / Skills

What to include: 10-20 relevant hard and soft skills organized in a bulleted or column format.

This is the section that matters most to the ATS. The Skills section is where keyword matching algorithms do their heaviest lifting. If a job description asks for "project management," "Agile," "JIRA," and "cross-functional collaboration," and those terms appear in your skills section, your resume gets a higher match score.

Best practices for 2026:

  • Use a mix of hard and soft skills. Technical skills (Python, Salesforce, SEO) + soft skills (leadership, negotiation, communication).
  • Include both acronyms and full terms. Write "SEO (Search Engine Optimization)" to catch both ATS variations.
  • Organize by category. Group related skills: Technical Skills, Management Skills, Language Skills.
  • Prioritize job-specific keywords. Pull 5-10 keywords from the job description and make sure they appear naturally in your skills section.

?? Skills Section Sample

Technical: Python, SQL, Tableau, AWS, Git, JIRA, Salesforce

Management: Project Management, Agile/Scrum, Team Leadership, Stakeholder Communication

Soft Skills: Cross-Functional Collaboration, Strategic Planning, Problem Solving, Data-Driven Decision Making

Section 4: Professional Experience

What to include: Work history in reverse chronological order with company name, job title, dates, and 4-6 bullet points per role.

This is your most important section for both ATS and human readers. The ATS looks for consistency in formatting, relevant keywords in your bullet points, and clear career progression. Recruiters scan this section to verify your experience matches their needs.

The perfect bullet point formula for 2026:

  • Action verb + what you did + how you did it + the result (quantified).
  • Example: "Led a cross-functional team of 12 to redesign the customer onboarding flow, reducing time-to-value by 40% and increasing NPS scores by 25 points."
  • 80% of your bullet points should include a number, percentage, or dollar amount.
ATS Tip: Use the company's exact name as it appears on LinkedIn or the company website. ATS systems sometimes cross-reference employer information, and mismatches can flag your resume.

Section 5: Education

What to include: Degree, institution name, graduation date, GPA (if 3.5+), and relevant coursework or honors.

The Education section is particularly important for entry-level candidates, recent graduates, and career changers. For experienced professionals, it becomes less critical but should never be omittedATS systems expect it.

Format:

  • Degree name (Bachelor of Science in Computer Science)
  • University name and location
  • Graduation month and year
  • GPA (only if 3.5 or above)
  • Honors (Cum Laude, Dean's List, etc.)
  • Relevant coursework (optional, mostly for recent grads)
ATS Tip: If you have a degree, spell it out fully: "Bachelor of Science in Marketing" not "B.S. Marketing." Some ATS systems miss abbreviations. Include both if space allows.

Section 6: Certifications & Professional Development

What to include: Relevant certifications, licenses, and completed training programs with issuing organization and date.

In 2026, this section has become a major ATS ranking signal. Many companies now require industry-specific certifications, and ATS systems are programmed to prioritize candidates who hold them.

Certifications that boost ATS scores in 2026:

  • PMP (Project Management Professional)
  • AWS Certified Solutions Architect
  • Google Analytics Individual Qualification
  • CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst) any level
  • SHRM-CP or PHR (HR professionals)
  • HubSpot Academy certifications
  • Six Sigma Green/Black Belt

Section 7: Additional Information (Optional But Recommended)

What to include: Languages, volunteer work, publications, patents, professional affiliations, or technical proficiencies not covered above.

This section is your competitive differentiator. While not strictly required, it can tip the scales in your favor when the ATS compare two closely matched candidates. Many ATS platforms now score this section for cultural fit and completeness.

Items that work well here:

  • Languages: "Fluent in Spanish (native), French (professional working proficiency)"
  • Volunteer experience: Especially leadership roles in professional organizations
  • Technical proficiencies: Software and tools not listed in your skills section
  • Professional memberships: IEEE, AIGA, AMA, etc.

Common ATS Resume Mistakes to Avoid

Even with all 7 sections in place, there are pitfalls that can get your resume rejected. Avoid these common mistakes:

  • Using tables or columns ATS parsers read left-to-right, top-to-bottom. Columns jumble the reading order.
  • Including images or icons ATS cannot read text inside images. No headshots, logos, or graphics.
  • Using non-standard fonts Stick to Arial, Calibri, Tahoma, or Georgia. Avoid script or decorative fonts.
  • Submitting in the wrong format PDF is best in 2026. DOCX is also accepted. Never submit PNG or JPG.
  • Keyword stuffing Include keywords naturally. Over-stuffing is detectable and can result in rejection.
  • Missing date ranges Every role needs start and end dates. Month and year format is standard.
  • Inconsistent formatting Use the same bullet style, font size, and heading format throughout.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need all 7 sections?

Yes, if you want to maximize your ATS pass rate. Each section serves a specific purpose in the parsing process. Omitting even one can reduce your match score by 10-15% in some ATS platforms.

What if I don't have certifications?

Skip the certifications section and add a "Professional Affiliations" or "Volunteer Work" section instead. Never include empty or irrelevant sectionsthat hurts your credibility.

Should I include a References section?

No. The phrase "References available upon request" is outdated and wastes valuable resume space. ATS doesn't score it, and recruiters assume you'll provide references when asked.

What's the best file format for ATS in 2026?

PDF is the most reliable format for preserving your resume layout while remaining ATS-readable. Most modern ATS platforms parse PDFs accurately. Save as "Standard" (not "PDF/A") to ensure compatibility.

How many pages should my resume be?

One page for candidates with less than 10 years of experience. Two pages for senior professionals with extensive experience. Never exceed two pagesATS systems may truncate or ignore content beyond the second page.
