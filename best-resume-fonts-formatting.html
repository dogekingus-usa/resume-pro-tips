The Best Resume Fonts for 2026

Choosing the right fonts can significantly impact how your resume is perceived by both human recruiters and applicant tracking systems (ATS). In 2026, certain font choices stand out as they offer a balance of professionalism, readability, and ATS compatibility. Here are some top picks:

  • Arial: A classic sans-serif font that is widely recognized by most ATS systems due to its simplicity and clarity.
  • Calibri: Another popular choice in the Microsoft Office suite, Calibri offers a clean look with good readability on screens.
  • Liberation Sans: This free font from Google is highly recommended for those who want to avoid proprietary software. It's clear and professional-looking.
  • Helvetica Neue: Known for its modern, sleek appearance, Helvetica Neue can be a strong choice if you are targeting industries that value contemporary design.

Optimal Font Sizes for Your Resume in 2026

The font size of your resume is crucial for ensuring it's not only readable but also easily processed by ATS systems. Here are the recommended sizes:

  • Main headings and subheadings (H1, H2): 14-18 pt
  • Body text: 10-12 pt
  • Contact information: 11-13 pt

These sizes ensure that your resume is legible while maintaining a clean, uncluttered appearance. Larger headings help draw attention to key sections of your resume, making it easier for both recruiters and ATS systems to quickly scan your document.

Elevating Your Resume Formatting in 2026

While font choices and sizes are important, the overall formatting of your resume also plays a significant role. Here's how you can enhance your resume's layout:

  • Consistent margins and alignment: Use standard margins (1 inch) to create a balanced look. Ensure that all elements align properly for a professional appearance.
  • Use of white space: Incorporate ample white space to avoid cluttering your resume and make it easier on the eye. This is particularly important when dealing with ATS systems, which need clean, easy-to-process documents.
  • Incorporate bullet points: Use bullet points for listing skills, experiences, and achievements to break up text and make information more digestible.

Promoting ATS-Friendliness in 2026

To ensure your resume gets past the initial screening by ATS systems, consider these additional tips:

  • Use keywords: Incorporate industry-specific keywords and technical terms relevant to the job you are applying for. Many companies use specific keyword filters.
  • Avoid special characters: Stick to standard characters to prevent issues with ATS systems that may not recognize non-standard symbols or punctuation.
  • Keep it simple: While design can be appealing, keep your resume basic and straightforward. Overly complex layouts might confuse the system and reduce the chance of being noticed by a human recruiter.

By carefully selecting fonts, using optimal font sizes, implementing clean formatting, and ensuring ATS-friendliness, you can create a highly effective resume that stands out in 2026. Remember, your resume is your first impression; it's crucial to make the right one!
