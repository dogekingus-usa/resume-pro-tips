Executive Resume Writing Tips for Senior Leaders

May 2026 · 9 min read · ' Resume Tips

Executive Resumes: A Different Standard

Executive resumes operate at a higher level than standard professional resumes. They emphasize strategic leadership, organizational impact, and vision rather than tactical execution. Board members, C-suite executives, and senior hiring managers read these resumes differently ' they want to see evidence of enterprise-level thinking.

What Executive Resumes Must Include

Executive Summary: A powerful summary of your leadership brand. 3-4 sentences that communicate your executive presence, industry expertise, and the scope of your leadership. "Transformational CTO with 20+ years driving digital innovation across Fortune 500 financial services firms. Led engineering organizations of 500+ through three successful digital transformations."

Strategic Impact: Each bullet point should describe impact at the organizational level, not the task level. "Developed five-year technology roadmap that reduced infrastructure costs by 40% while enabling 3x engineering velocity."

Leadership Scope: Include the size of teams you've led, budgets you've managed, P&L responsibility, and reporting structure. "Led 200-person global engineering organization with $50M operating budget, reporting directly to CEO."

Key Differentiators

  • Focus on results, not responsibilities
  • Include board service, speaking engagements, and industry recognition
  • Keep it to 2 pages max (3 for very senior roles)
  • Use executive-level language and avoid tactical jargon
