Why Resume Writing Has Changed in 2026

Before diving into the step-by-step process, it's important to understand the current landscape. Here's what's different about resume writing in 2026:

  • AI-first screening: Nearly all large and mid-sized employers use AI to parse, rank, and filter resumes. Your resume must be formatted for machine readability.
  • Skills-based hiring: More companies are removing degree requirements and focusing on demonstrated skills. Your resume must prominently feature relevant competencies.
  • Hybrid and remote keyword optimization: With remote work here to stay, resumes need to signal self-management, async communication, and digital collaboration skills.
  • Shortened attention spans: Recruiters spend an average of 7.4 seconds scanning a resume. You need to make an impact instantly.

Step 1: Choose the Right Resume Format

The format you choose sets the foundation for everything. In 2026, three resume formats dominate:

FormatBest ForATS Score
ChronologicalCareer professionals with steady work history⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
FunctionalCareer changers and those with employment gaps⭐⭐⭐
Hybrid / CombinationMost job seekers — balances skills + experience⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

For most job seekers in 2026, the hybrid (combination) format is the optimal choice. It places a skills summary at the top followed by reverse-chronological experience — giving you the best of both worlds.

Step 2: Write a Strong Header

Your resume header must include:

  • Full name — First and last, no nicknames
  • Phone number — With area code
  • Professional email — firstname.lastname@gmail.com (not partyguy99@hotmail.com)
  • LinkedIn URL — Customized with your name
  • Location — City, State (or "Remote" if fully remote)
  • Portfolio/GitHub (optional) — If relevant to your field

Step 3: Write a Powerful Professional Summary

Your professional summary (also called a resume summary or profile) is the first thing recruiters read. In 2026, it's also the first section AI compares against the job description. Write 2-4 sentences that cover:

  • Your title and experience level (e.g., "Senior Marketing Manager with 8+ years of experience")
  • Your top 2-3 skills or specializations
  • A major achievement with a metric
  • What you're looking for (optional, but helpful for alignment)

Example:
"Results-driven Project Manager with 6+ years of experience leading cross-functional teams in SaaS environments. Reduced project delivery times by 30% while improving client satisfaction scores to 95%. Seeking to drive operational excellence at a scaling technology company."

Career changers and entry-level candidates should use a career objective instead. Read our comparison of resume summary vs. objective to decide which is right for you.

Step 4: List Your Work Experience with Impact

This is the most important section of your resume. In 2026, employers care less about what you did and more about what you accomplished.

The CAR Method (Challenge → Action → Result)

For every bullet point under each role, use this framework:

  • Challenge: What problem did you face?
  • Action: What did you do to solve it?
  • Result: What was the measurable outcome?

Weak: "Responsible for social media management"
Strong: "Overhauled social media strategy after 40% engagement decline, implementing data-driven content calendar that increased engagement by 215% and generated 12,000+ new followers in Q1 2026."

Quantify Everything

Numbers grab attention. Include percentages, dollar amounts, time saved, and any other metrics. Our complete guide on how to quantify achievements on your resume provides 50+ before-and-after examples.

Step 5: Showcase Your Skills Strategically

In 2026, skills are more important than ever. Here's how to structure this section:

Hard Skills (Technical)

List tools, technologies, certifications, and methodologies relevant to your target role. Match these to keywords in the job description.

Soft Skills (Power Skills)

Communication, leadership, problem-solving, adaptability — these are increasingly valued in the age of AI. Back them up with examples in your experience section.

ATS Keywords

Modern ATS systems scan for specific keywords. The best way to optimize is to analyze job descriptions in your target role and incorporate the key terms naturally. For a comprehensive list by industry, check our guide on ATS resume keywords for 2026.

Step 6: Education & Certifications

List your highest degree first. Include:

  • Degree name and field of study
  • Institution name and location
  • Graduation year (or "Expected 2027" if in progress)
  • Relevant certifications with issuing body and date

Pro tip: If you have more than 5 years of experience, keep the education section brief. If you're a recent graduate or career changer, expand it with relevant coursework, projects, and GPA (if 3.5+).

Step 7: Optional Sections That Add Value

Depending on your field and experience level, consider adding:

  • Projects: Portfolio links, GitHub repos, side projects
  • Languages: Include proficiency level (e.g., "Fluent in Spanish")
  • Volunteer work: Demonstrates leadership and community involvement
  • Awards & honors: Only include recent, relevant recognitions
  • Publications & speaking: Great for academics, consultants, and thought leaders

ATS Optimization Tips for 2026

To ensure your resume gets past AI screeners:

  1. Use standard section headers — "Work Experience," not "Where I've Worked"
  2. Submit as PDF (unless the job posting specifies otherwise)
  3. Include a keywords section naturally throughout, not as a list at the bottom
  4. Avoid tables, columns, and graphics — ATS systems struggle to parse them
  5. Use standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Inter
  6. Match job description language — If they say "managed," don't say "oversaw"

Resume Writing Checklist for 2026

Before submitting your resume, verify every item:

  • ✅ Contact info is correct and professional
  • ✅ Professional summary is tailored to the target role
  • ✅ Bullet points use the CAR method with quantified results
  • ✅ ATS keywords from the job description are incorporated naturally

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a resume be in 2026?

One page for early-to-mid career (under 10 years). Two pages max for senior roles with 10+ years of experience. No recruiter reads page three.

Should I include a photo on my resume?

No — not in the US, UK, Canada, or Australia. It can lead to unconscious bias and causes ATS parsing issues. Include a photo only if applying in countries where it's standard (Germany, parts of Asia).

What's the best file format for submitting a resume?

PDF is best for preserving formatting. Use Word (.docx) only if the job posting specifically requests it. Never submit a PNG or JPEG.

How do I write a resume with no experience?

Focus on education, projects, volunteer work, internships, and transferable skills. Emphasize your potential and willingness to learn. Our guide for recent graduates covers this in depth.

Should I use a resume template?
