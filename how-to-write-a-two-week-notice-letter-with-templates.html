How to Write a Two-Week Notice Letter (With Templates)

October 6, 2023

Writing a two-week notice letter is an essential step when leaving your job. It shows professionalism and respect for your employer's time while giving you the opportunity to wrap up your responsibilities gracefully. This guide will walk you through the process, including tips on how to write effectively and templates to get you started.

Why Send a Two-Week Notice?

A two-week notice is more than just a formality; it serves several important purposes:

  • It provides time for your employer to find and train a replacement.
  • It allows you to finish any ongoing projects or tasks that need completion before leaving the company.
  • It gives colleagues the chance to transition smoothly into their roles, ensuring continuity in operations.

Key Elements of a Two-Week Notice Letter

Your letter should be concise and professional. Here are the key elements you need to include:

  1. Salutation: Address your employer by name, if known, or use a formal greeting such as "To Whom It May Concern."
  2. Date of Submission: Clearly state the date on which you are submitting this notice.
  3. Your Name and Title: Include your full name and job title to ensure clarity.
  4. Notice Period: State that you will be providing a two-week notice, as per company policy or agreement.
  5. Rationale (Optional): If applicable, briefly explain why you are leaving the position. Keep this brief and to the point.

Actionable Tips for Crafting Your Notice Letter

  • Avoid using negative language or making demands about severance packages in your notice letter, as this could damage your professional reputation.
  • Remain positive and appreciative of the opportunities provided by the company during your tenure. This reflects well on you and can leave a good impression for future reference.
  • Offer assistance with the transition process to make it smoother for both parties involved.

    Templates for Your Two-Week Notice

    Here are two templates to help guide your writing. Choose one that best fits your situation.

    Standard Two-Week Notice Letter Template

    

                [Date]

                To Whom It May Concern,

                I am writing to formally provide my notice of resignation from [Company Name] as of [Last Day]. I have decided to leave the company after [number of years/months], and I will be serving a two-week notice period.

                

                During my time here, I have greatly appreciated the opportunities provided by your organization. I am grateful for the skills I've gained and the support I received from my colleagues.

                

                As we transition, I would like to offer assistance in wrapping up any ongoing projects or tasks that require completion. Please let me know how I can contribute during this period.

                

                Thank you for understanding as I move on to new opportunities. I wish your company all the best and hope to remain in touch professionally.

            

                Sincerely,

                Your Name

    Shorter Two-Week Notice Letter Template

    

                [Date]

                [Manager's Name],

                I am writing to inform you that my last day of employment will be [Last Day]. As per company policy, I am providing a two-week notice.

                

                Please let me know how I can assist during this transition period. Thank you for the opportunities and support provided during my time here.

            

                Sincerely,

                Your Name

    By following these guidelines and using one of our templates, you'll be able to write a professional two-week notice letter that leaves a positive impression on your employer. Remember, this is also an opportunity to maintain good relationships and continue networking within the industry.
