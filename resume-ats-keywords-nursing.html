EHR System Keywords: The Hidden Gatekeeper

Electronic Health Record (EHR) system proficiency is one of the most underrated keyword categories for nursing resumes. Hospital ATS systems frequently include EHR experience as a weighted filter. A nurse proficient in a specific system costs the hospital less in training time, and ATS algorithms reflect this preference.

  • EPIC âEUR" The most-requested EHR in US hospitals. Approximately 55% of large hospitals (250+ beds) use EPIC. If you have EPIC experience, list the specific modules: EPIC ASAP (ED), EPIC Stork (OB), EPIC Beacon (oncology), EPIC OpTime (OR), EPIC Willow (pharmacy)
  • Cerner âEUR" Second most common. Used by major hospital chains including HCA and Kaiser Permanente. Keywords: Cerner PowerChart, Cerner Millennium, Cerner Soarian
  • Meditech âEUR" Common in community hospitals and long-term care facilities. Keywords: Meditech Expanse, Meditech Magic
  • Allscripts âEUR" Used in outpatient and physician practice settings
  • Athenahealth âEUR" Growing in ambulatory and telehealth nursing roles

Format tip: In your skills section, write: "Electronic Health Records: EPIC (ASAP, Beacon, OpTime), Cerner PowerChart, Meditech." This gives the ATS exact phrases to match while showing breadth of experience.

How to Structure a Nursing Resume for ATS Parsing

Even the best keywords fail if your resume structure confuses the ATS parser. Hospital ATS systems are notoriously strict about formatting. Follow this section-by-section blueprint for maximum parsing accuracy.

1. Professional Summary (3-4 Lines)

Include your specialty, years of experience, top certification(s), and primary clinical skill. This section carries the highest ATS weight.

ER Nurse Example:
"Compassionate Registered Nurse (RN) with 6+ years of Emergency Room experience. CEN and ACLS certified with proven expertise in triage assessment, trauma resuscitation, and code management. Proficient in EPIC ASAP and Cerner systems. Demonstrated ability to manage 12+ high-acuity patients per shift with rapid clinical decision-making under pressure."
ICU Nurse Example:
"Critical Care Registered Nurse (RN) with 8 years of ICU experience. CCRN and ACLS certified with advanced competency in ventilator management, hemodynamic monitoring, CRRT, and titratable drip titration. Skilled in EPIC charting and ABG interpretation. Managed 2:1 and 1:1 patient ratios in a 24-bed medical-surgical ICU."

2. Skills Section (Scannable, No Graphics)

Use a clean, single-column list format. Group keywords under subheadings that match how the ATS categorizes them.

Clinical Skills: Patient Assessment · Ventilator Management · Hemodynamic Monitoring · Wound Care · IV Therapy · Medication Administration · EKG Interpretation · Phlebotomy · Emergency Response · Pain Management · Palliative Care

Specialty Procedures: Central Line Care · Arterial Line · Chest Tube Management · CRRT · Code Management · Triage Protocol · Port Access · Chemo Administration

EHR Systems: EPIC (ASAP, Beacon) · Cerner PowerChart · Meditech · Allscripts

Certifications: RN · BSN · CCRN · ACLS · BLS · PALS · TNCC

Soft Skills: Multidisciplinary Collaboration · Patient Advocacy · Crisis Communication · Precepting · Family Education · Discharge Planning

3. Experience Section (Bullet Points with Keywords in Context)

Each bullet point should contain 1-2 specialty-specific keywords embedded naturally. Use numbers and patient ratios to add weight.

Weak (generic): "Provided nursing care to patients in the ICU."
Strong (keyword-rich): "Managed care for 2:1 ICU patients including ventilator management, titratable vasopressor titration, and hemodynamic monitoring via arterial line and CVP. ACLS certified with active participation in 15+ code blue resuscitations."

Common Nursing Resume Keyword Mistakes

  • Overusing "compassionate" without clinical keywords: Soft skills matter, but they should never replace clinical keywords. Every soft skill should be paired with a clinical competency.
  • Listing every certification you have ever earned: Only list current, active certifications. Expired certifications create credibility gaps. ATS systems can cross-reference expiration dates for major certs like ACLS and BLS.
  • Using outdated terminology: "Nurse's notes" is outdated. Use "clinical documentation" or "EHR charting." "Bedside care" is weak âEUR" use "direct patient care" paired with a specialty term.
  • Ignoring the job description: If the job posting mentions "EPIC Beacon" and "chemotherapy administration," those exact phrases must appear in your resume. Mirror the language of the job description precisely.
  • Using tables or columns: Hospital ATS parsers are among the most fragile. Tables, columns, text boxes, headers/footers, and graphics will cause parsing errors that bury your keywords.
  • Omitting patient ratios and volume: Including your patient-to-nurse ratio and average patient volume per shift is a strong implicit keyword engagement strategy that ATS algorithms in 2026 are trained to detect and weight positively.
