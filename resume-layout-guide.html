Best Resume Layout: Formatting Tips That Pass ATS Systems

The job search process can be daunting, but a well-crafted resume is your first step towards landing that dream job. An effective resume not only showcases your skills and experience but also ensures it passes through Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to reach the hands of hiring managers. This article provides comprehensive guidelines on creating an ATS-friendly resume layout and formatting.

ATS-Friendly Fonts and Font Sizes

When choosing fonts for your resume, it is crucial to select those that are easy to read and compatible with most ATS systems. Here are the best options:

Font Description
Arial A classic sans-serif font that is widely supported by most ATS systems.
Courier New A monospaced font ideal for technical resumes or those with code snippets.
Helvetica A clean, modern sans-serif font that is also widely supported by ATS systems.
Liberation Sans An open-source alternative to Arial and Helvetica that works well with most ATS systems.

The recommended font size for your resume should be between 10-12 points. This ensures readability on both screen and print, while still keeping the document concise and professional.

Recommended Margins and Spacing

To ensure your resume is easy to read and formatted correctly, follow these guidelines:

  • Margins: Aim for at least 0.5 inches on all sides of the page. This allows for better readability and a clean look.
  • Line Spacing: Use double-spacing between lines of text. Single spacing can make your resume appear cluttered, especially when ATS systems process it.
  • Paragraph Spacing: Add a line or two of space between paragraphs to separate them visually.

These settings ensure that your resume is not only visually appealing but also passes through ATS systems without issues.

Best Section Order

The order in which you list sections on your resume can significantly impact its effectiveness. Here's the recommended sequence:

Section Description
Contact Information Your name, email address, phone number, and LinkedIn profile (if relevant).
Career Objective or Summary A brief introduction of your professional goals and qualifications.
Professional Experience List your job titles, company names, dates of employment, and key responsibilities and achievements for each position. Use bullet points to highlight your accomplishments.
Educational Background Your degrees, institutions attended, graduation dates, and any relevant coursework or research projects.
Skills List your technical skills, software proficiency, certifications, and any other relevant abilities. Consider using a skills matrix to ensure you align with the job requirements.
Awards and Honors (if applicable) Highlight any recognitions or accolades received during your career.

This order ensures that your most relevant information is easily accessible to both human readers and ATS systems. Tailor this sequence slightly based on the job you are applying for, emphasizing sections that align with the job requirements.

PDF vs DOCX File Formats

The choice between PDF and DOCX formats depends on several factors:

  • PDF: Ideal for final submission as it preserves formatting and is easily shared. However, it may not be editable once submitted.
  • DOCX: Best for initial drafts or when you need to make changes later. It allows for easy editing but may lose formatting when submitted through ATS systems.

To ensure your resume looks professional and consistent, consider using a PDF format for final submissions. This can be achieved by exporting your DOCX file as a PDF before submitting it to potential employers or uploading it online.

Design Elements to Avoid

A visually appealing resume is important, but avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Creative Fonts and Color Schemes: Stick to simple, ATS-friendly fonts and neutral color schemes. Overly complicated designs can cause your resume to be flagged or rejected by ATS systems.
  • Unnecessary Graphics and Images: While images can add personality, they are not necessary for a professional resume and may cause formatting issues in some environments.
  • Large Text Boxes and Tables: Use these sparingly as they can make your document look cluttered. Opt for bullet points to present information clearly and concisely.

Focusing on simplicity ensures that your resume is both visually appealing and functional, increasing its chances of passing through ATS systems successfully.

ATS Testing Tools and Simulators

To ensure your resume passes through ATS systems effectively, use these tools:

  • Jobscan (https://www.jobscan.co): A comprehensive tool that checks your resume against job descriptions to identify keywords and suggest improvements.
  • ResumeGenius (https://www.resumegenius.com/ats-testing-tool): Provides feedback on how well your resume will perform in different ATS systems.
  • Resumers (https://resumers.com/): Offers a detailed analysis of your resume, including readability and ATS compliance.

Using these tools can help you optimize your resume for specific job applications, ensuring that it meets the requirements of both human readers and ATS systems.

Mobile-Friendly Resume Tips

In today's digital age, many employers review resumes on mobile devices. Here are some tips to ensure your document is accessible and user-friendly:

  • Keep It Simple: Avoid overly complex layouts or large images that can be difficult to read on smaller screens.
  • Use Clear Headings: Ensure headings and subheadings are easy to spot, using bold text or larger font sizes if necessary.
  • Optimize for Scrolling: Organize content in a way that makes it easy to read when scrolling vertically. Use bullet points and short paragraphs to break up information.

A mobile-friendly resume not only looks good on screens but also ensures that your application is accessible from anywhere, increasing the chances of being seen by potential employers.
