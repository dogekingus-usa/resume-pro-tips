Resume Summary Examples That Get Interviews in 2026

Your resume summary is the first thing recruiters read. It is your 3-line pitch that determines whether they keep reading or move to the next candidate. Here are proven summary templates that get results.

The Formula

A great summary follows this structure: your title and years of experience, your key skills and expertise, your most impressive achievement with numbers. Example: "Senior Marketing Manager with 8+ years driving growth for B2B SaaS companies. Expert in demand generation, content marketing, and conversion optimization. Generated $4.2M in pipeline revenue through integrated campaigns." This summary tells the recruiter exactly who you are, what you can do, and what you have achieved.

Industry Examples

Tech: "Full-Stack Engineer with 5 years building scalable web applications. Proficient in React, Node.js, Python, and AWS. Designed and deployed a microservices architecture that reduced API response times by 60%." Finance: "CPA with 10 years of experience in corporate accounting, financial reporting, and audit management. Led SOX compliance for a Fortune 500 company with zero material weaknesses." Healthcare: "Registered Nurse with 7 years of critical care experience. Skilled in emergency response, patient advocacy, and team leadership. Reduced ICU readmission rates by 25% through improved discharge planning."

Common Mistakes

Vague statements ("hard-working team player"). Lists of skills without context. First-person language ("I am a..."). Generic summaries that could apply to anyone. An objective statement instead of a summary. Your summary should differentiate you. If it could apply to 100 other candidates, rewrite it.
