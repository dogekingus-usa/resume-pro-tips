Skills for Resume: The Complete List of Top Skills Employers Look For

Creating an effective resume is crucial to landing the job you desire. One of the most important elements on a resume is the skills section, which can make or break your chances with employers. In this article, we will explore both hard and soft skills, providing detailed insights into what employers are looking for in each category.

Hard Skills vs Soft Skills Explained

Best Resume Formats by Industry
Resume Keywords By Industry

Hard skills and soft skills are two distinct types of competencies that can significantly impact your resume's effectiveness. Understanding the difference between them is essential for tailoring your resume to meet employer expectations.

Hard Skills

Hard skills refer to specific, measurable abilities or technical knowledge required for a particular job. These are often industry-specific and can be listed as bullet points under relevant sections of the resume. Examples include programming languages, software proficiency, certifications, and professional licenses.

Soft Skills

Soft skills, on the other hand, refer to personal attributes that influence how well you interact with others. These are less tangible but equally important in determining your suitability for a role. Examples include communication, leadership, teamwork, adaptability, and problem-solving.

How to List Skills (Format Section)

The way you list your skills can significantly impact their visibility and effectiveness. Here's how to format them for maximum impact:

  1. Categorize Your Skills: Group hard and soft skills separately or in related categories.
  2. Use Action Verbs: Start with strong action verbs like "implemented," "managed," or "created."
  3. List Specific Examples: Provide clear examples to demonstrate your proficiency. For instance, instead of saying "Problem-solving skills,-- say "Solved customer complaints by providing solutions within 24 hours.--
  4. Quantify Achievements: Where applicable, include numbers or percentages to quantify results. For example, "Increased sales by 15% in six months."

Certifications That Boost Skills Section

Certifications not only validate your skills but also demonstrate a commitment to professional development. Here are some popular certifications:

  • ITIL Foundation Certification: For IT professionals.
  • Project Management Professional (PMP): Ideal for project managers.
  • Certified Public Accountant (CPA): Essential for finance and accounting roles.
  • Microsoft Certified: Azure Developer Associate: For tech professionals working with Microsoft cloud services.

Skills-Based Resume Format

A skills-based resume focuses on your abilities rather than just the job history. Here's a simple structure:

  1. Contact Information
  2. Summary/Objective Statement: A brief introduction highlighting key skills.
  3. Skills Section (organized by category):
    • Hard Skills
    • Soft Skills
  4. Professional Experience: Include only relevant work experience that showcases these skills.
